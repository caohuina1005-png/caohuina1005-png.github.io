The QFM project was not a dashboard upgrade — it was the reconstruction of Chewy's entire quality performance governance foundation. The legacy QPD had lost organizational trust, caused fulfillment centers to build shadow tracking tools, and left leadership without a reliable network-wide view across 40 KPIs.

The Problem — Three layers of failure

  • Data integrity breakdown: KPI logic had drifted from business reality over time; stakeholders couldn't trust the numbers
  • Network alignment collapse: individual FCs built their own local tools with inconsistent metric definitions, making cross-site comparison impossible
  • Decision speed: the flat, undifferentiated layout forced managers to manually scan hundreds of cells to find issues

What I built

  • Audited and revalidated all 40 KPIs against current business logic through structured stakeholder conversations across 5 operational domains (Building, Inbound, Outbound, Inventory Control, Replan)
  • Rebuilt the 5,000-line monolithic SQL script into a modular dbt architecture on Snowflake — each metric isolated in its own model with incremental processing and full lineage visibility
  • Designed a three-tier performance classification system (Healthy / Watch / Critical) encoding judgment directly into the reporting layer
  • Built a role-stratified Tableau workbook: executive heatmap for rapid network scan, operational drill-down for in-week issue identification, and analyst-level single-metric deep dive

Outcome

  • Stakeholder feedback: "a thousand times better" than the legacy QPD with documented instances of the dashboard directly driving FC-level operational decisions
  • Restored a single trusted source of truth across the fulfillment network, eliminating shadow tracking fragmentation
  • Random Defect Rate — a SOX-compliance metric — now produced by a governed, auditable pipeline

Defect Workflow Risk Attribution Engine

Random Counts are one of the most labor-intensive inventory control mechanisms in fulfillment — but the organization had no systematic way to explain why defects were happening or which transaction paths were injecting risk. Everything was intuition-driven.

The key conceptual shift

  • Reframed the unit of analysis: instead of treating each defect as a standalone event, each random count became the end of a workflow window — the exposure period between two clean count events
  • This enabled workflow-level modeling, not just event-level counting

What I built

  • Designed a workflow fact table capturing all transactions within each exposure window, including both source and destination location joins to eliminate blind spots in inbound attribution
  • Applied probabilistic attribution: each defective workflow distributes 1 unit of defect exposure across transaction types by their share of activity — avoiding false precision
  • Built three layered metrics: Defect DPMO (primary risk ranking), Confidence-Weighted DPMO (structural risk accounting for attribution ambiguity), and Risk Persistence Index (detection timing dimension)
  • Engineered performance using pre-aggregation, staging tables, and incremental logic for Airflow compatibility

Outcome

  • First statistically grounded defect risk ranking across all transaction paths in the network
  • Shifted the organization from intuition-based defect management to normalized, risk-based prioritization
  • Directly supported strategic goal of reducing random counts by ~70% through smarter, evidence-based sampling
Operational System Design · Business Impact
MNR Control Tower — Middle-Mile Visibility System
$76MAnnualized savings
5-dayMaturity window logic
Network→ operator level

Shipments were being marked "shipped" in the warehouse system but in some cases never entered the carrier network. From a customer view: orders appeared in transit with no movement. From an operations view: no visibility or accountability at the warehouse-to-carrier handoff, no standardized metric, and no way to quantify the scale of the problem.

Problem definition & metric design

  • Worked with stakeholders to define two MNR rules: shipments with only "Out for Collection" tracking events, and shipments with 120+ hour gaps between tracking updates
  • Identified a critical false-positive issue: newly shipped packages were being incorrectly flagged due to tracking delays; resolved by introducing a 5-day maturity window

What I built

  • Designed and implemented a full SQL-based data pipeline integrating shipment data, carrier tracking events, process path info, packaging profiles, and manual handling flags
  • Built a hierarchical dashboard enabling analysis from network level down to fulfillment center, process path, profile type, manual vs. automated handling, and individual operator
  • Designed the dashboard as a diagnostic tool — not just a reporting view — so stakeholders could identify where issues were happening and why

Outcome

  • Clearly surfaced that manual handling flows consistently had higher MNR rates than automated sorter flows, enabling targeted process interventions
  • Improved transparency at the warehouse-to-carrier handoff and strengthened carrier performance management with data-backed insights
  • Shifted the organization from reactive issue handling to proactive monitoring — establishing a trusted, standardized metric for ongoing performance tracking
